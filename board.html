Meet Our Board

Tian Griffin

Artistic Director

Tian Griffin began dancing at age three in Los Angeles, California. She continued her pre-professional training at Pasadena Dance Theatre and attended numerous summer intensives, including American Ballet Theatre. In addition, Tian has danced professionally with Pasadena Dance Theatre, California Contemporary Ballet, at the Pasadena Dance Festival, and has choreographed numerous pieces including “River” which won a choreographic award. She joined CUBE her first semester at Barnard and has danced numerous roles including Medora in Le Corsaire and served as a repetiteur last year. Tian is a junior and is thrilled to be serving as the Artistic Director of CUBE in 2020.

Abigail Melbourne

Executive Director

Abby Melbourne is a sophomore at Barnard studying Political Science and Philosophy. She trained for 13 years at her local Iowa studios and attended summer intensives at the Rock School for Dance Education, Joffrey Ballet, and American Ballet Theatre. In addition to her balletic pursuits, she is the Lead Senior Editor for the Columbia University Journal of Politics and Society, a Writing Fellow, and a Resident Assistant. She previously served as CUBE’s Assistant Executive Director for the 2019 season, and is excited to continue serving the Columbia dance community as CUBE’s Executive Director.

Michaela Fleischer

Assistant Artistic Director

A second-semester sophomore at Barnard College, Michaela began dancing at age two, studying ballet and contemporary at a pre-professional level. She attended numerous dance intensives, including the Bolshoi Ballet Academy, Joffrey Ballet School, Travis Wall Experience, and Joe Tremaine Dance. Michaela has also choreographed her own contemporary solos and performed several classical variations at YAGP and other national competitions. Aside from CUBE, she has choreographed for Orchesis, CoLab, CMTS, and KCST. Michaela’s previous roles with CUBE include Little Swans in Swan Lake, Toreador Lead in Don Quixote, and Odalisque in Le Corsaire. She is so excited to be Assistant Artistic Director this year!

Madeline Mariani

Assistant Executive Director

Madeline Mariani is a sophomore planning on majoring in Medicine, Literature, and Society. She started her ballet training at age 12 and has received training from Ruth Page School of Dance, Hubbard Street Dance Chicago, and Thodos Dance Chicago. Madeline performed with CUBE during her first semester in Fall 2018, and then joined CUBE board as a repetiteur and later as Financial Director. She is so excited to continue with CUBE as Assistant Executive Director.

Camila Vicioso

PR Director

Camila Vicioso grew up in NYC and trained at the School of American Ballet. She also attended summer programs including Boston Ballet, Miami City Ballet, and Chautauqua Dance. Between high school and college, she took a gap year to dance with Connecticut Ballet. She is now a first year at Columbia and is undecided on her major. She is very excited to continue being a part of CUBE and to join the board as Publicity Manager.

Pazit Schrecker

Web Manager

Pazit (Pazi) is a junior at Barnard studying Computer Science and taking Ballet classes when she can. She started dancing ballet at age 12 after doing gymnastics and acrobatics and began pointe a year and a half after that. Pazit danced with Erin Gottwald at egdance, at Aika Ballet School, and the French Academie of Ballet in New York City. When she's not dancing, at the Spectator office, or doing homework, she is probably baking vegan muffins while listening to a true crime podcast. She joined CUBE in her first semester of sophomore year after transferring to Barnard and is excited to be on the CUBE Board this semester as the Web Manager!

Jenna Everard

Event Coordinator

Jenna Everard is a sophomore at Barnard College, studying Environmental Biology and Computer Science. She grew up in Kirkland, Washington where she trained in ballet, contemporary, jazz, and tap for 13 years at Dance Time, Premiere Dance Center, and Elizabeth’s Dance Dimensions. This is Jenna’s second semester dancing with CUBE, with her first role as part of the Court Corps in Cinderella. Aside from CUBE, Jenna has danced with Orchesis and Columbia UnTapped, plays and performs classical piano, and is a writer for the Columbia Science Review. She is very excited to be on the CUBE board as the Event Coordinator!

Gloria Lu

Gloria started her experience with CUBE her first semester at Barnard. CUBE is a great family to her and she is looking forward to the performances next year.

Elizabeth Gardner

Costumer

Lizzie Gardner is a sophomore at Barnard College studying history and dance. She has performed in CUBE's productions of Don Quixote and Le Corsaire, and looks forward to taking on more responsibility as costumer for the Spring 2020 production. She grew up in the suburbs of Washington, DC and received most of her technical training at the Ballet Academy in Beltsville, Maryland. Emma Chen

Repetiteur

Emma Chen is a second-semester junior at Barnard College majoring in psychology and minoring in economics. She joined CUBE in fall 2017 as a freshman and this is her third semester as a repetiteur. At age four, Emma began learning ballet at Logrea Dance Academy in Westchester, NY. In 2008, Emma moved to Beijing, China where she continued dancing at the Beijing Academy of Dance, J-Ballet, and finally On Pointe Dance Studio. There, she participated in vocational ballet examinations from the Royal Academy of Dance (RAD) and modern dance examinations from the Imperial Society of Teachers of Dancing (ISTD). During high school, Emma also choreographed for her school’s dance team and volunteered teaching young local Chinese students ballet. As a dancer in CUBE, Emma has performed in The Nutcracker, Swan Lake, and Don Quixote.

Dale Padelford

Repetiteur

Dale Padelford is a current student in the Graduate Foundations Program of the School of Professional Studies. In 2014, she graduated from the Columbia University School of General Studies with a degree in Sustainable Development. Dale had the privilege to perform in Columbia University Ensemble’s premiere production of the Sleeping Beauty and their more recent production of Le Corsaire. Lorelei Robinson
