Meet Our Board

Tian Griffin

Co-Director

Tian Griffin began dancing at age three in Los Angeles, California. She continued her pre-professional training at Pasadena Dance Theatre and attended numerous summer intensives, including American Ballet Theatre. In addition, Tian has danced professionally with Pasadena Dance Theatre, California Contemporary Ballet, at the Pasadena Dance Festival, and has choreographed numerous pieces including “River” which won a choreographic award. She joined CUBE her first semester at Barnard and has danced numerous roles including Medora in Le Corsaire and served as a repetiteur in 2019 and as the Artistic Director in 2020. Tian is a senior and is thrilled to be serving as Co-Director of CUBE in 2021.

Michaela Fleischer

Co-Director

A second-semester sophomore at Barnard College, Michaela began dancing at age two, studying ballet and contemporary at a pre-professional level. She attended numerous dance intensives, including the Bolshoi Ballet Academy, Joffrey Ballet School, Travis Wall Experience, and Joe Tremaine Dance. Michaela has also choreographed her own contemporary solos and performed several classical variations at YAGP and other national competitions. Aside from CUBE, she has choreographed for Orchesis, CoLab, CMTS, and KCST. Michaela’s previous roles with CUBE include Little Swans in Swan Lake, Toreador Lead in Don Quixote, and Odalisque in Le Corsaire. She is so excited to be Assistant Artistic Director this year!

Camila Vicioso

Assistant Artistic Director

Camila Vicioso grew up in NYC and trained at the School of American Ballet. She also attended summer intensives including Boston Ballet, Miami City Ballet, and Chautauqua Dance. Between high school and college, she took a gap year to dance with Connecticut Ballet. She is now a sophomore at Columbia and is majoring in Psychology with a concentration in Dance on a pre-med path. She is very excited to continue being a part of CUBE and to be the Assistant Artistic Director of CUBE.

Li Bing Appel

Assistant Press and Donor Relations Director (PDR)

Li Bing Appel is a first year at Barnard College. She began her ballet training at age four at Chrystie Street Ballet Academy in New York City. Li Bing then received professional training at Ballet Tech, the New York City Public School for Dance. She has performed with Rioult Dance at the Joyce Theater. Li Bing joined CUBE in her first semester at Barnard and is delighted to be CUBE’s Assistant PDR Director.

Jenna Everard

Assistant Executive Director

Jenna Everard is a sophomore at Barnard College studying Environmental Biology and Computer Science. She grew up in Kirkland, Washington where she trained in ballet, contemporary, jazz, and tap for 13 years at Dance Time, Premiere Dance Center, and Elizabeth’s Dance Dimensions. In college, Jenna dances with CUBE and Orchesis, plays and performs classical piano, and writes for the Columbia Science Review. She previously served as CUBE’s Event Coordinator in the fall and is thrilled to now serve as the Assistant Executive Director of CUBE this semester!

Gloria Lu

Costumer

Gloria Lu is a current first year student at Barnard. She has been a ballet dancer since the age of three years old. Her favorite ballet is Don Quixote. She also loves watching movies and shopping! Gloria started her experience with CUBE her first semester at Barnard. CUBE is a great family to her and she is looking forward to the performances next year.

Pazit Schrecker

Web Director

Pazit (Pazi) is a senior at Barnard studying Computer Science and taking Ballet classes when she can. She started dancing ballet at age 12 after doing gymnastics and acrobatics and began pointe a year and a half after that. Pazit danced with Erin Gottwald at egdance, at Aika Ballet School, and the French Academie of Ballet in New York City. When she's not dancing, at the Spectator office, or doing homework, she is probably baking vegan muffins while listening to a true crime podcast. She joined CUBE in her first semester of sophomore year after transferring to Barnard and is excited to be on the CUBE Board this semester as the Web Manager!

Christine Blackshaw

Repetiteur

Christine Blackshaw is a junior in SEAS studying civil engineering. She grew up in Baltimore, MD, and trained at the Peabody Preparatory and at summer intensive programs, including ABT NY, Kaatsbaan Extreme Ballet, and Pennsylvania Ballet. She has performed in several dance groups while at Columbia University, working with renowned company members and choreographers. Christine joined CUBE in her sophomore year and is excited to be a part of 'building' this semester's production as a rep.

Emma Chen

Repetiteur

Emma Chen is a second-semester senior at Barnard College majoring in psychology and minoring in economics. She joined CUBE in fall 2017 as a freshman and this is her fifth semester as a repetiteur. At age four, Emma began learning ballet at Logrea Dance Academy in Westchester, NY. In 2008, Emma moved to Beijing, China where she continued dancing at the Beijing Academy of Dance, J-Ballet, and finally On Pointe Dance Studio. There, she participated in vocational ballet examinations from the Royal Academy of Dance (RAD) and modern dance examinations from the Imperial Society of Teachers of Dancing (ISTD). During high school, Emma also choreographed for her school’s dance team and volunteered teaching young local Chinese students ballet. As a dancer in CUBE, Emma has performed in The Nutcracker, Swan Lake, and Don Quixote, and A Midsummer Night's Dream

Grace Miner

Repetiteur

Grace Miner began dancing at the age of five at Providence Ballet School and continued her training there while performing with Providence Ballet Theatre. She also attended summer intensives in New York at Joffrey Ballet and American Ballet Theatre. Filip Pryzbecien

Hannah Richmond

Lorelei Robinson

Repetiteur

Lorelei Robinson is a junior in Columbia College. She is pursuing a major in history and hopes to apply to medical school after graduation. She danced in Cincinnati, Ohio, for over 15 years while growing up. Most of her training was with Cincinnati Ballet Academy, where she enjoyed several productions with Cincinnati Ballet. Her senior year of high school, she danced full time as a trainee there, finishing high school in the mornings and online in the evenings. Besides CUBE, Lorelei enjoys runs and walks in City parks and exploring NYC with friends. She also enjoys being a member of One for the World Columbia. She loves CUBE for the people and the awesome productions it puts on!
